Types of Anemia

Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA)
  1. Etiology: This disorder may result from chronic blood loss, inadequate intake, or increased physiological demand for iron (e.g. pregnancy).
  2. Symptoms: Most common presentation of fatigue，pallor and pica (typically with mineral manifestation).
  3. Management: Usually managed with iron supplementation as well as changes in diet to include more high-iron foods – such as leafy greens and red meats.
Vitamin Deficiency Anemias
  1. Vitamin B12 Deficiency: Most commonly due to inadequate intake or malabsorption syndromes, treatment is replacement vitamin B12.
  2. Folate Deficiency—Common among pregnant women/ alcohol use disorder; use folic acid
Aplastic Anemia
  1. Causes: This is due to failure of the bone marrow, which might be a result of autoimmunity or exposure to nephrotoxic agents.
  2. management – transfusions of blood; transplant of the bone marrow
Hemolytic Anemias
  1. Sickle Cell Anemia and Thalassemia – MAINTAINED: Human-Disposed
  2. Genetic: Autoimmunetic origin may be susceptible to immunosuppressive therapy and splenectomy may help
Anemia of chronic disease (ACD)
  1. a Associated Conditions: These are most commonly seen as part of chronic infections or inflammatory states.
  2. Management: Disease-modifying therapy and/or erythropoiesis stimulating agents
