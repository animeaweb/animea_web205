Overview:

Anemia is a problem caused by not having enough healthy red blood cells or haemoglobin to carry oxygen to the body's tissues. Haemoglobin is a protein found in red blood cells that delivers oxygen from the lungs to other organs of the body. Anemia can cause weak , and short of breath. There are many types of anemia. Each has its own reason. They range in intensity from simple to sharp. Anaemia can be a warning sign of a serious illness. Treatments for anaemia, taking supplements or undergoing medical procedures. Following a healthy diet may prevent certain types of anaemia.
